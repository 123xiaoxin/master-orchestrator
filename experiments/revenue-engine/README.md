# Revenue Engine Experiment

This experiment turns Master Orchestrator into a cash-flow support system.
It is separate from the industrial safety track.

## Goal

Create short-cycle, low-risk service deliverables that can fund compute,
testing, and continued framework development.

The first revenue target is not live industrial control. Industrial control has
long sales cycles, high trust requirements, and high liability. The early
revenue lane should sell planning, review, documentation, workflow setup, and
agent-pack customization.

## Principles

1. Sell deliverables, not promises.
2. Keep humans in control of all outbound contact and final delivery.
3. Do not claim verified ROI unless there is evidence.
4. Do not touch real equipment, production systems, secrets, or customer
   accounts during early experiments.
5. Treat every output as draft material until reviewed by the engineering
   cluster.

## First Sellable Offers

| Offer | Buyer | Deliverable | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| AI workflow audit | Solo founders, small teams, creators | Workflow map, bottleneck list, 3-agent plan, setup notes | Low |
| Agent pack setup | Developers and operators using Codex/OpenClaw-style tools | 1-5 agent templates, start prompt, runbook, scorecard | Low |
| Industrial agent safety review | Industrial software teams, automation vendors, consultants | Proposal-only safety architecture review and redline checklist | Medium |
| Research/report pack | Consultants, agencies, product teams | Structured report, source notes, executive summary, delivery deck outline | Low |

## Dual-Cluster Split

| Cluster | Machine | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Test cluster | Other computer | Draft market research, offer variants, report samples, segmented execution plans |
| Engineering cluster | This computer | Validate claims, package templates, review safety, produce final deliverables |

The test cluster can draft. The engineering cluster decides what becomes a
deliverable.

## Default Delivery Loop

1. Pick one buyer segment.
2. Generate a narrow offer and acceptance criteria.
3. Produce a sample deliverable.
4. Run reality-check and safety review.
5. Package final files.
6. Record what worked in the run log.

## Do Not Do Yet

- Do not build a SaaS before there is paid demand.
- Do not sell live device control.
- Do not use unverified industrial customer claims.
- Do not let agents send outreach without human approval.
- Do not promise cost savings, uptime gains, or ROI as guaranteed outcomes.
